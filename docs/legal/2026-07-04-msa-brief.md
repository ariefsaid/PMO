# MSA / subscription-agreement brief — for legal counsel

**Status:** Director draft 2026-07-04, owner to take to Indonesian counsel. This is the input
brief describing the commercial + operational reality the contract must encode — NOT a contract.
Decisions referenced: ADR-0047 (topology), ADR-0048 (ERPNext), `docs/backlog.md` GTM program.

## 1. Parties & product

- Vendor: [legal entity TBD — owner to confirm entity name/NPWP before drafting].
- Client: a contract-/project-based company or **group of companies** (subsidiaries covered
  under ONE agreement — the "Organization"; see glossary: Entity).
- Product: PMO Portal, a hosted SaaS project/procurement operations platform, provided as a
  **dedicated isolated environment per client** (own database instance, own URL). Optionally
  bundled with a dedicated ERPNext accounting instance (ADR-0048) — the bundle should be a
  schedule/addendum, not baked into the core MSA.

## 2. Services & scope

- Subscription to the hosted software, per-client environment.
- **White-glove onboarding** (included or priced separately — owner decision): environment
  provisioning, data import of active state + up to 1 year summary-grade history, per-role
  walkthrough sessions. Historical data imported "as provided"; vendor not responsible for
  source-data accuracy.
- Support: **WhatsApp group per client**, business-hours response target of [X hours — owner
  pick, suggest 4 business hours], best-effort after hours. Public status page (BetterStack)
  referenced as the uptime/incident communication channel.
- Explicitly OUT of scope: custom development, accounting/bookkeeping services, tax advice,
  statutory filings (client's accountant owns period-close/e-Faktur in ERPNext).

## 3. Fees & billing (manual — no payment processor)

- Subscription fee: [pricing TBD] invoiced [monthly/quarterly/annually — suggest quarterly or
  annual up-front at this stage], paid by bank transfer against invoice, net [14/30] days.
- **AI credits:** subscription includes [N] credits/month for the in-app Assistant; additional
  credits purchasable. Contract should let credit pricing/allowance change with [30] days notice
  (pricing is being calibrated during pilot — launch phase may run credits unmetered; the
  agreement should permit metering to switch on with notice).
- Late payment: suspension right after [30] days notice; no data deletion on suspension.

## 4. Data — the clauses clients will actually read

- **Client owns its data.** Vendor gets only the license needed to operate the service.
- **Export anytime:** self-service export (CSV/XLSX) exists in-product; on termination, vendor
  provides a full export within [30] days, then deletes client data after [60/90] days.
- **Isolation:** each client runs in a dedicated environment (no shared database with other
  customers) — safe to state contractually, it's architecturally true (ADR-0047).
- **Hosting location:** managed cloud (Supabase/AWS) in [Singapore — confirm region per client
  project at provisioning; current staging is Sydney]. No Indonesian data-residency commitment
  in v1 — counsel to advise UU PDP posture for B2B operational data as processor/pengendali
  wording requires.
- **AI processing disclosure:** the Assistant sends user prompts + the minimum necessary data
  context to third-party LLM providers (via OpenRouter) to answer requests; no client data is
  used to train models; vendor staff do not read Assistant conversation content (aggregates
  only — owner-locked policy).
- Confidentiality: mutual, standard.
- Backups: daily, [7]-day retention (managed platform); disaster-recovery commitment =
  restore from most recent backup (implicit RPO 24h) — do NOT promise tighter without PITR.

## 5. Service levels — keep it honest at MVP

- Uptime target [99.5]% monthly, measured by the public status page; **remedy = service
  credits only** [suggest: 1 day credit per 0.5% below target, capped], no cash penalties.
- Planned maintenance windows with [24h] notice excluded.
- No SLA on the AI Assistant's answer quality/latency (third-party model dependency) — only on
  the platform being reachable.

## 6. Liability, IP, warranties

- Liability cap: [12] months of fees paid; exclude indirect/consequential; carve-outs per
  counsel (confidentiality breach, IP infringement).
- IP: vendor owns the software and all improvements; client owns its data; feedback license to
  vendor. No source-code rights (note: the repo being public on GitHub is a distribution
  posture question — **flag to counsel**: consider licensing terms of the public repo vs the
  hosted service).
- Software provided "as is" beyond the express SLA; no fitness-for-purpose warranty on
  financial figures — the ERPNext ledger (client-operated) is the accounting system of record
  (ADR-0048), PMO displays are operational views.

## 7. Term & termination

- Initial term [12] months, auto-renew [annually] unless [60] days notice.
- Termination for convenience by client with [60] days notice [owner: allow or not — suggest
  allow after initial term; it's a trust signal at this stage].
- Termination for cause (breach uncured [30] days, insolvency).
- Post-termination: export + deletion per §4.

## 8. Counsel flags (please advise)

1. **UU 24/2009 language law** — agreement with Indonesian parties should have a Bahasa
   Indonesia version (dual-language, BI prevails or EN prevails — advise).
2. UU PDP processor obligations for B2B operational data incl. employee PII (user accounts,
   timesheets) — what privacy-policy + consent language the product pages need.
3. Stamp duty / e-Meterai for digital signing.
4. Governing law Indonesia, dispute resolution [BANI arbitration vs district court — advise
   cheapest credible option].
5. Whether the ERPNext bundle (open-source software operated for the client) needs its own
   liability language (GPL software, vendor as operator not licensor).
